The First Computers



Before the invention of the modern computer there were many different stages to its inception. One of the first things that relate to the computer is the abacus which was a tool that aided people with computations like counting large numbers or addition. The antikythera mechanism was a greek invention that is the oldest known mechanical analog computer and it was used to predict astronomical positions and eclipses. The first design of a modern computer was the analytical engine which was designed by Charles Babbage and although it wasn't made it is still credited as being the first design of a modern computer. The world's first electronic digital computer was the Atanasoff-Berry computer which was built by John V. Atanasoff, a physics and mathematics professor, and Clifford Berry, a graduate engineering studant. Konrad Zuse had developed Z3 which was the world's first functional program-controlled computer and Zuse had continued to develop many different computers. The first actual computer bug was a moth that had gotten stuck in between some computer parts.
